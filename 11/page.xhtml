The rabbit carried on his task diligently and carefully and looked forward to that years’ harvest. The field was ready when the badger came back to his home, and he was surprised that he could play the rabbit for a fool so easily. The rabbit, on the other hand, was not surprised at all when the badger shirked his duty once again. Summertime was the time of watering the fields, but the badger preferred to go to the village and visit the fair. 

The seeds sprouted, the plants grew, and the whole field was covered by leaves of all shapes and sizes. This was the time of weeding. The rabbit looked at every single plant, adjusted the earth around them and cut the weed off. He was angry at the badger because he had not worked a single day since spring and fall was next to come. To avoid weeding the field, the badger pretended to be in ill health and only showed up to say that he would help the rabbit as soon as he got better - but that never happened.
