One day in spring, the badger had an idea. He asked his neighbour if he would agree to put the two crop fields together and share the harvest when fall came. This would be better for both because the harvest would be larger, and both would have more to eat during winter. The rabbit who was already composting his field put his tools aside and pondered about the badger’s proposition. The rabbit knew that the badger didn’t work on his crop field hard enough and that every year he ended up looking for food in the nearby forest. 

The rabbit suspected that the badger would deceive him. So, the rabbit came up with a counterproposal. He agreed to put the two crop fields together and to share the harvest when fall came, but when it came to sharing the harvest, they would have to choose whether they wanted to take the part of the plant that grew in the earth or above the earth.
